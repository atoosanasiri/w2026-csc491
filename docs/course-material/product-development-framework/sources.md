## Sources & Bibliography
{: .section-header }

### Essential Texts

**1. Torres, T. (2021). *Continuous Discovery Habits*. Product Talk LLC.**

Framework for weekly evidence-based product decisions. Documents how teams at Amazon, Netflix, and Spotify use opportunity solution trees to map user pain points to feature experiments.

**2. Richardson, C. (2018). *Microservices Patterns*. Manning Publications.**

Strangler Fig pattern, logical vs. physical decomposition. Shows how to evolve from monolith → service-oriented → event-driven without rewriting.

**3. Ries, E. (2011). *The Lean Startup*. Crown Business.**

Build-Measure-Learn loop, validated learning gates. Formalized the feedback loop used at IMVU, Dropbox, and Zappos.

**4. Cagan, M. (2017). *Inspired: How to Create Tech Products Customers Love*. Wiley.**

Product discovery vs. delivery cadence. Defines the dual-track agile model where discovery and delivery run in parallel.

**5. Blank, S. (2013). *The Four Steps to the Epiphany*. K&S Ranch.**

Customer development framework for startups. Establishes the distinction between searching for a business model and executing one.
